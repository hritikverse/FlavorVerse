Gajar Ka Halwa

Recipe Details

cold winters bring hot delicacies to the Indian plates & its incomplete without the sinful desserts like #GajarKaHalwa.
A soft pudding made with carrot & enhanced with cardamom, flaked almonds or cashews, this is your must-try dessert.

Ingredients

650 grams of grated carrot or 8 to 9 carrots
4 cups of full-fat milk
4 to 5 tablespoon of ghee (clarified butter)
180 to 190-gram sugar or add as per requirement
1 teaspoon cardamom powder
10 to 12 roughly chopped cashews
10 to 12 roughly chopped almonds
1 to 2 tablespoon raisins (Kismis)
1 pinch saffron strands (optional)

Instructions

Firstly, 8 to 9 carrots and wash them very well, then peel them and grate them. you can likewise
grate them with a handheld grater or you can you any food processor. you have to must grate before
cooking it donâ€™t grate it earlier otherwise the color will be converted into brown.

Now take a Kadai or thick bottomed pan or nonstick pan. add milk and grated carrot into that.

Mix them well and stir them continuously on low flame. now bring the whole mixture of carrot
and milk to a boil and then let it simmer.

You have to keep stirring it while the mixture is shimmering, otherwise, it will start sticking
in the bottom of the pan. and keep the flame on low heat.

You can see that grated carrot will start to cook in the milk and the milk will starts to reduce and evaporate.

At the point when the milk has 75% decreased, add the ghee, sugar, and powdered cardamom to the
combination.

Mix them well and continue to simmer and cook on low heat.

keep cooking until the milk has been reduced completely. at the end add cashews, almonds, saffron,
and raisins. mix well and after 3 to 4 seconds turn off the stove.

Gajar Ka Halwa is ready and serves it hot.

Serving Suggestion: You can serve Gajar Ka Halwa hot or cold both. some people like to it eat hot
and some like to eat it chilled. Garnish it with some chopped dry fruits while serving it.
